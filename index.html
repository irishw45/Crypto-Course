Lesson 1: Intro to Trading & Markets

Trading is essentially the act of buying and selling assets with the goal of making a profit. Markets are where this happens—whether it’s the stock market, crypto market, or futures market. What’s important to understand first is that every market has buyers and sellers, and prices are constantly changing based on supply and demand. You’re not trying to predict the future—you’re learning to recognize patterns and probabilities. Think of trading as a game of probabilities, not certainties.

Key Terms:

  • Market
  • Exchange
  • Volatility
  • Trader
  • Liquidity

Lesson 2: Understanding Crypto & Blockchain

Crypto is short for cryptocurrency—digital money that lives on a technology called the blockchain. The blockchain is basically a database that’s shared across many computers, making it hard to tamper with. Unlike banks or credit card companies, crypto doesn’t rely on middlemen. It’s peer-to-peer, which means people can trade directly with one another. This opens up a whole new kind of finance. If you’re trading crypto, you’re not just watching charts—you’re participating in an emerging digital financial system.

Key Terms:

  • Blockchain
  • Decentralization
  • Token
  • Wallet
  • Ethereum
